Michelle is a Product Designer

A 15 Year Journey into UI Design

2003

MySpace launched and a passion was ignited.

I loved being able to make my MySpace profile page look and feel exactly like me so I taught myself as much HTML & CSS as I could stuff into that About Me text box.

03-07

I kept up my curiosity for design and tech in high school.

I became captain of the robotics team and filled my class schedule with electives like Computer-Aided-Design, Photography, Painting, and Computer Repair.

That's me, the robotics nerd

07-11

I spent 4 years exploring interests at the University of Kansas - Rock Chalk!

I spent my freshman year as a computer engineering major, but I was often the only girl in my classes and never really felt like I belonged. I also found myself jealous of my roommates’ liberal arts electives.

After leaving engineering, I explored a few majors and ended up with a broad liberal arts education with focuses in Psychology, Design, Sociology, and Communications.

13-17

I moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in marketing.

Interacting with customers IRL at Beautycon Festival 2016

I found myself in the fast-paced world of cosmetics. I loved finding out what our customers wanted and designing campaigns around their needs, but I always felt like something was missing.

In early 2017, I was marketing manager for the brand Styli-Style. Our website needed a refresh, but it wasn’t in the budget so I took on the project myself. As soon as I began diving into the code to make my design changes I was hooked and realized I had rediscovered a passion I hadn’t felt since those early days of MySpace.

2018

Mentor-led UX/UI Design Apprenticeship at Bloc.io.

I enrolled in the UX/UI Design program at Bloc in late 2017, where I’m working on design projects end-to-end from research to development. I’ve finally found that perfect balance between creative and technical, and I’m excited to start this next chapter in my life.

To be continued...

My Design Process

Research

Define the problem, set the scope of work, gather user research, and perform competitive analysis.

Explore

Explore solutions through sketches and low fidelity wireframes. Get user feedback on prototypes.

Adjust

Using the test data, refine the wireframes to create high fidelity mockups, test and iterate again if needed.

Develop

Turn static mockups into a functional prototype or a finished product through frontend development.
