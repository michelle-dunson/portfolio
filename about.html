A 15 Year Journey into Design

  • 2003

    MySpace launched and I quickly became obsessed with customizing my profile page. I loved making it look and feel exactly like me so I taught myself as much HTML & CSS as I could stuff into that About Me text box.

  • 2003-2007

    I kept up my curiosity for both tech and design in high school, becoming captain of the robotics team and filled my class schedule with electives like Computer-Aided-Design, Photography, and Computer Repair.

  • 2007

    I started college at the University of Kansas (Rock Chalk!) following the technical path as a computer science major, but I was often the only girl in my classes and never really felt like I belonged. I also found myself wanting a more creative field.

  • 2008-2011

    After leaving engineering, I explored a few majors and ended up with a broad liberal arts education with focuses in Psychology, Design, Sociology, and Communications.

  • 2013-2016

    After college I moved to Los Angeles and pursued a marketing career in the fast-paced world of cosmetics. I loved finding out what our customers wanted and designing campaigns around their needs, but I always felt like something was missing.

  • 2017

    In early 2017, I was marketing manager for the brand Styli-Style. Our website needed a refresh, but it wasn’t in the budget so I took on the project myself. As soon as I dove into the code to make my design changes I was hooked. I had rediscovered a passion I hadn’t felt since those early days of MySpace.

  • 2018

    This realization led me to the UX/UI Design program at Bloc.io where I’m working on design projects end-to-end from research to development. As a Product Designer I’ve finally found that perfect balance between creative and technical, and I’m excited to start this next chapter in my life.
